<p><ul><li>Serology Test</li><li>Stool Antigen Test</li><li>Urea Breath Test</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Helicobacter pylori non-invasive testing market comprises three main types: serology tests, stool antigen tests, and urea breath tests. Serology tests detect antibodies in blood samples, indicating past or current infection. Stool antigen tests identify H. pylori directly from fecal samples, offering an accurate diagnosis. Urea breath tests involve the ingestion of a labeled urea solution, which H. pylori metabolizes, leading to a detectable change in breath composition. Together, these methods provide efficient, non-invasive options for diagnosing H. pylori infections.</p></p>
