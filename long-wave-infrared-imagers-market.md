<p><p>The Long-Wave Infrared Imagers market comprises two primary types: unrefrigerated and refrigerated imager systems. Unrefrigerated long-wave infrared imagers utilize ambient temperature sensors, making them cost-effective and suitable for applications like surveillance and temperature monitoring. In contrast, refrigerated long-wave infrared imagers use cooling systems, providing higher sensitivity and image quality, making them ideal for demanding applications such as defense and industrial inspections. Both types play crucial roles across various sectors, including security, automotive, and medical diagnostics.</p></p>
