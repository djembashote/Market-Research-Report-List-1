<p><p>Molten Carbonate Fuel Cells (MCFC) are electrochemical devices that convert chemical energy from fuels, typically natural gas or biogas, into electrical energy with high efficiency. Operating at elevated temperatures (around 600 to 700 degrees Celsius), MCFCs utilize a molten carbonate salt as the electrolyte, facilitating the oxidation of fuels and the reduction of oxygen, thereby generating electricity, water, and carbon dioxide as byproducts. This technology offers advantages such as fuel flexibility, high efficiency, and the ability to use waste heat for cogeneration purposes, making it suitable for large-scale power generation and industrial applications.</p><p>The MCFC market is experiencing significant growth, driven by increasing demand for clean energy solutions and a shift towards sustainable power generation technologies. With growing awareness of climate change and the need for low-carbon energy sources, investments in fuel cell technology are rising.
