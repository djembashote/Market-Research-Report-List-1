<p><p>The organic cassava flour market is primarily divided into two types: sweet cassava flour and bitter cassava flour. Sweet cassava flour, derived from sweet cassava varieties, is commonly used in gluten-free baking and cooking due to its mild taste and versatility. In contrast, bitter cassava flour, made from bitter cassava, contains higher cyanogenic compounds and requires processing to remove toxins. It is less commonly used but may find niche applications in traditional dishes or health products. Both types cater to diverse consumer preferences and dietary needs.</p></p>
