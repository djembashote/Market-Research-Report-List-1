<p><p>PU resins are key materials in the faux leather market, primarily categorized into dry-process and wet-process synthetic leather. Dry-process synthetic leather involves a coating of polyurethane over a substrate, creating a lightweight, durable material suitable for various applications. In contrast, wet-process synthetic leather involves immersing the substrate in a liquid PU solution, resulting in a softer, more flexible product. Both types offer distinct advantages in texture and durability, catering to different consumer preferences and end-use applications.</p></p>
