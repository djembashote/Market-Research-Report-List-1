<p><p>Sustained release ocular drug delivery systems enhance the therapeutic effectiveness of ophthalmic medications. Liposomes encapsulate drugs within lipid layers for extended release. Microcapsules and nanocapsules create a protective coating around active substances, enabling controlled release. Microspheres and nanospheres are small particles that maintain drug release over time, improving bioavailability and reducing dosing frequency. Implants are solid or semi-solid devices placed in the eye, providing sustained drug exposure, minimizing side effects, and increasing patient compliance in ocular treatments.</p></p>
